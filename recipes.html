Everlasting Syllabub

An Original 18th Century Recipe (From The Art of Cookery Made Plain and Easy by Hannah Glasse)

Ingredients Amounts
Heavy Cream (or double cream) 1 1/4 pint (590ml)
Medium sweet wine such as white port, sherry, etc. 1/4 pint (120ml)
Juice of orange and large lemon 1 each
Powdered sugar 1/2 pound (227g)
Orange blossom water 1 tsp

Directions

  1. Mix the cream and sugar together in a large bowl until sugar is dissolved
  2. Whisk in the remaining ingredients
  3. Whisk either by hand for 30 minutes or with an electric mixer until you form stiff peaks
  4. Serve or refrigerate for up to 3 days

A little history on Syllabub...

Syllabub was originally a simple drink of sweet wine and frothed cream popularized in the early 16th century. The cream would usually separate from the wine and rise to the top (similar in appearance to that of a root beer float). However, the centuries went on, much more sugar was added to syllabub in order to stabilize it and cause the cream and wine to stay together - thus creating everlasting syllabub.

Everlasting syllabub was then even further perfected by famous English victorian cook, Hannah Glasse who at her age was a sort of Julia Childs (and was also known worldwide despite living in the 1700s all the way from the American colonies to India). This everlasting syllabub is her method made airy and sweet. Enjoy!
